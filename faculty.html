The MST Program Coordinator

Annalee S. Hadsall
Associate Professor 7


Associate Professor Annalee S. Hadsall serves as the UPLB Museum of National History's curator overseeing orchids and epiphytes. Within the Plant Biology Division of the Institute of Biological Sciences at UPLB, she holds the position of Associate Professor 7. Prof. Hadsall specializes in plant systematics and holds a Master's degree in Botany from UPLB, which she obtained in 1985. Her undergraduate education includes a Bachelor's degree in Biology from the Philippine Normal College, where she graduated cum laude. Prof. Hadsall's research focuses on various areas, including exploring the morpho-anatomical diversity of native orchids in the Philippines, conducting biodiversity studies and conservation efforts in mountain ecosystems, and examining phytogeographical patterns of epiphytes. Recently, she was honored with a UP Faculty Grant Award in Botany (Plant Systematics) for her exceptional contributions to teaching and research. This recognition was given for her outstanding performance in the 2019-2021 One UP Professorial Chair and Faculty Grant System.



Past MST Program Coordinators

Rosemarie D. Eusebio
Associate Professor
August 2019- July 2023

She was a respected figure in the world of physics education research. Her dedication to improving teaching methods through assessment and evaluation, flipped classroom techniques, and quality assurance was unmatched. But her impact extended far beyond her professional achievements. Known as a loving mother and teacher, she played multiple roles in the lives of her students – a guide, a friend, and a mentor. In her spare time, Prof. Eusebio found solace and joy in playing table tennis, a hobby that brought her both relaxation and a sense of camaraderie. Her unwavering commitment to her students and her passion for her work made Prof. Rosemarie a truly inspirational figure in the academic community.

Liza C. Carascal +
Associate Professor

She was one of the pioneers of University of the Philippines Open University (UPOU). When UPOU was established in 1995, she served as the first University Registrar and established the admissions, registrations, records, graduation, exams and assignment, and electronic data processing sections. She also institutionalized the policies and procedures of the UPOU Office of the University Registrar. In 2000, she was called back to UPLB, her mother unit, to serve as Principal of the UP Rural High School but she remained with UPOU as Program Chair for the Diploma in Mathematics Program from 2000 to 2002, and the “manager” of the UPOU Chorale.
