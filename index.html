Four Areas of Specialization

BS in Mathematics and Science Teaching (BS MST) program is composed of four areas of specialization.

Biology

Institute of Biological Sciences

Biology is the scientific discipline that explores the diversity, structure, function, and interactions of living organisms, elucidating the principles and processes that underlie life itself.

Chemistry

Institute of Chemistry

Chemistry is the scientific study of matter, its properties, composition, and the changes it undergoes, which ultimately govern the behavior and interactions of all substances in the universe.

Mathematics

Institute of Mathematical Sciences and Physics

Mathematics is the abstract study of numbers, quantity, structure, space, and patterns, providing a universal language and toolset for understanding and describing the fundamental relationships and laws that govern the universe.

Physics

Institute of Mathematical Sciences and Physics

Physics is the branch of science that deals with the fundamental principles governing the behavior of matter and energy, and their interactions in the physical world.
